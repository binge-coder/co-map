What is Outcome-Based Education?

Outcome-Based Education (OBE) is a student-centered approach to education that emphasizes the achievement of specific, measurable learning outcomes. Rather than focusing solely on what is taught, OBE prioritizes what students are expected to learn and be able to do after completing a course or program.

The core philosophy of OBE is that all educational activities should align with the desired learning outcomes, ensuring that students acquire the necessary skills, knowledge, and attitudes by the end of their academic journey.

In an OBE system, learning outcomes are predefined and clearly stated for each subject or course. These outcomes are typically classified into Course Outcomes (COs), Program Outcomes (POs), and Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs). Continuous assessment and feedback mechanisms are essential components of OBE, enabling educators to evaluate how well the students are meeting the outcomes and to adapt teaching strategies accordingly.

The implementation of OBE fosters accountability and transparency in the educational process.
